01 — Foundations

Principles before pixels.

KD is the brand of a designer who improves how businesses work, not just how they look. Every design decision in this system exists to prove that promise. When in doubt, come back to these four principles.

Principle 01

Clarity is the product

Remove ambiguity before adding decoration. If an element doesn't make the message clearer, it doesn't ship. Generous space, one idea per section, copy that says what it means.

Principle 02

One blue, used with intent

Electric Blue is the only brand colour. It marks what matters: the accented word, the active state, the call to action. Scarcity is what gives it power — never dilute it.

Principle 03

Editorial, not corporate

The brand reads like a well-set magazine: large confident headlines, quiet uppercase labels, warm paper backgrounds. Pill geometry softens the grid without making it playful.

Principle 04

Performance is non-negotiable

Low-carbon, fast, accessible. One typeface, system-light CSS, motion that respects prefers-reduced-motion. The brand must live what it sells: efficiency without compromise.

02 — The Mark

Two letters, one chevron.

The KD mark fuses the K and D into a single directional form: an ink wedge meeting an electric blue chevron, always pointing forward. It is drawn on a 18.62 × 11.73 grid and is never redrawn, re-proportioned or re-coloured outside the approved variants.

Primary mark — Ink + Electric Blue. The ink half inherits the theme's text colour; the blue half never changes. assets/kd-mark.svg

Variants

Four approved renderings. The duotone mark is the default everywhere; fall back to mono only when colour reproduction is limited (engraving, single-colour print, favicons at tiny sizes).

Clearspace & minimum size

Keep a protected zone of half the mark's height on all sides. Minimum height: 14px digital (as used in the site footer), 5mm print. Below that, use the single-glyph favicon files.

clearspace = ½ × mark height

Misuse

The fastest way to weaken the mark is to "improve" it. Don't.

Don't rotate or flip the mark.

Don't stretch or re-proportion it.

Don't recolour outside approved variants.

Don't add shadows, outlines or effects.

Motion signature

The mark has one signature animation — the two halves slide in from opposite sides and settle together (the site preloader). Use it only as an opening moment, never on loop in UI. In expressive contexts (showreels, playground) the mark may spin on the Y axis with a blue glow.

.pre-ink  { animation: pre-in-left  0.9s 0.15s var(--kd-ease) both; }

04 — Typography

One typeface, many voices.

Everything is set in Instrument Sans — display, body, labels. Hierarchy comes from size, weight and tracking, not from mixing families. Display sizes tighten to −0.02em at weight 500; labels open up to +0.14em in uppercase.

The accent em

Let's start your journey.

The brand's typographic signature: in any display heading, the single most important word is wrapped in <em> and renders in Electric Blue, upright (never italic). One accent per heading, usually the last word.

Weights

Regular 400 — body copy

Medium 500 — all display

Semibold 600 — buttons, card titles

Bold 700 — case-study client names

Fallback stack: "Instrument Sans", "Helvetica Neue", Arial, sans-serif. Loaded from Google Fonts as a variable font (ital + wght 400–700).

Setting rules

Do

  • Balance headlines — text-wrap: balance, max ~18ch for hero statements.
  • Cap measure — body 62ch, ledes 30em, supporting copy 52ch.
  • Colour hierarchy — headings in Text, paragraphs in Muted.
  • Use sentence case everywhere except Label/Tag roles.

Don't

  • Introduce a second typeface — ever.
  • Use true italics for emphasis; the blue em is the emphasis system.
  • Set display sizes at weight 400 or 700 — display is always 500.
  • Track body copy; tracking belongs to labels and tags only.

06 — Motion

Settle, don't bounce.

All motion shares one personality: elements rise, unblur and settle — like a print coming into focus. One easing curve drives everything. Nothing bounces, nothing spins (except the mark, when it's earned).

cubic-bezier(0.22, 0.7, 0.2, 1) — fast out, long soft landing. Every eased transform on the brand uses this curve.

The house ease at 900ms.

Rise + unblur

The signature entrance: translateY(2rem) + blur(5px) → settled.

Choreography: page-load elements stagger at +150ms steps (kicker → title → sub → index). Hover feedback is immediate; entrances are slow. Under prefers-reduced-motion every animation is removed, not shortened.

08 — Imagery

Work first, always in motion.

Imagery is the work itself — mockups, screens, campaigns — ideally moving. No stock photography, no decorative imagery. Three treatments keep it consistent:

1 · The hero scrim

I design Brand Identity for Consumers

Full-bleed media always carries the three-layer ink scrim (radial centre + 200° linear + left edge) so Paper White type stays legible on any footage. Statement pattern: label kicker + balanced display line.

2 · Placeholder art

Missing media is never a grey box: deep-blue radial gradient (blue 45% top-left, deep #01145A bottom-right) with the client's initial, italic, at 18% paper opacity.

3 · The portrait treatment

People are shown grayscale (contrast 1.05) with an Electric Blue colour-blend tint at 90% — the brand's one photographic filter. Portraits may be clipped by the chevron geometry of the mark.

Media rules

Do

  • Video-first — muted, looped, playsinline, lazy-loaded with a poster.
  • Lock aspect ratios — 3:2 cards, 16:9 reels, 4:5 portraits.
  • Round every frame — 18px, media never touches a sharp corner.

Don't

  • Use stock photography or generic "office" imagery.
  • Put text on media without the ink scrim.
  • Autoplay with sound, anywhere, ever.

09 — Voice & Tone

Say it straight. Prove it works.

KD speaks in first person, in short declarative sentences, about outcomes. The voice of a senior practitioner who does the work himself: confident without hype, warm without chat.

Direct

"I make businesses clearer, more consistent, and easier to choose." Verbs first, outcomes second, no adverbs doing the heavy lifting.

Evidence-led

Claims come with receipts: 15 years, 35 case studies, 4 continents. If a number can carry the sentence, let it.

Practitioner-first

"I", never "we". KD is one person who stays hands-on from first workshop to final deployment — the copy should sound like it.

Performance-framed

Design is discussed as a business instrument: clarity, positioning, conversion, equity. "Identities, not logos." "Strategy comes before style."

Vocabulary

Say

  • perform / performance — the brand's spine word
  • clarity, consistency, positioning, touchpoint
  • identity systems (never "logo design")
  • built, shipped, deployed — maker verbs
  • Irish English spelling: colour, organisation, optimise

Avoid

  • Agency-speak: "passionate", "award-winning", "synergy", "solutions provider"
  • Hedging: "we believe", "we strive to", "world-class"
  • Exclamation marks and emoji in brand copy
  • "We" — there is no we

10 — Accessibility

Performance includes everyone.

Accessibility is part of the performance promise, not a compliance chore. These are the brand's non-negotiables:

Focus is loud

Every interactive element gets a 3px Electric Blue outline, offset 3px, on :focus-visible. A blue skip-link drops in on first Tab.

Motion is optional

prefers-reduced-motion removes the preloader, reveals, marquees and parallax entirely — content appears settled, never animated slower.

Contrast holds in both themes

Body text ≥ AA in light and dark. Blue is display-only on dark backgrounds (see §03). Errors always pair colour with a written message.

Semantics carry the design

Real buttons, labelled icon controls (aria-label), alt text on client logos, decorative marquees hidden with aria-hidden. Touch targets ≥ 40px.
